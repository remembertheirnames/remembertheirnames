130. This is the number of people within just the
U.S. that lose their lives to opioids each day.





Since 1999, over 700,000 people have died
from drug overdoses.

2 out of 3 drug overdose
deaths involves an opioid.

This equates to about half a million lives
taken by opioids.





While heroin is perhaps the most widely
infamous opioid, about 80% of heroin users
first start from misuse of prescription
opioids.

Why, then, has prescription opioid misuse
been so widespread and unregulated? How is this tied to the legacy left by Arthur M. Sackler?

How can we remember the suffering and void left by those who have passed as a result of this epidemic, and what can we do to permanently improve institutional practices moving forward?
